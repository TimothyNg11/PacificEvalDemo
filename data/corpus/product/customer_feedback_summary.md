# Customer Feedback Summary — Q3 2024

## Overview

This report summarizes customer feedback collected during Q3 2024 through multiple channels: NPS surveys (1,024 responses), customer support tickets (3,847 tickets), customer advisory board meetings (2 sessions), and 15 in-depth customer interviews.

## Net Promoter Score (NPS)

Our Q3 NPS is **47**, up from **42** in Q2 2024 and **38** in Q1 2024. The improvement is driven by increased satisfaction with search relevance following the hybrid search launch.

### NPS by Segment
- **Enterprise**: 52 (up from 46 in Q2)
- **Mid-Market**: 45 (up from 41 in Q2)
- **SMB**: 38 (down from 39 in Q2)

### NPS by Region
- **North America**: 49
- **Europe**: 44
- **Asia-Pacific**: 41

## Top Feature Requests (by frequency)

1. **Advanced Analytics Dashboard** — 127 requests
   "We need to show ROI to our leadership team. Without usage analytics, it's hard to justify the subscription cost." — VP of Operations, Fortune 500 customer

2. **Mobile App** — 98 requests
   Customers want to search and view documents on mobile devices. Currently, the mobile web experience is limited.

3. **Custom AI Models** — 76 requests
   Enterprise customers want to bring their own fine-tuned models for domain-specific search. Particularly relevant for legal and healthcare verticals.

4. **Slack/Teams Integration** — 71 requests
   Users want to search Meridian documents directly from their messaging tools without context-switching.

5. **Bulk Document Upload** — 63 requests
   Current upload limit is 100 documents per batch. Several customers need to upload 10,000+ documents during initial onboarding.

## Churn Analysis

Q3 churn rate: **4.2%** (annualized), down from **5.1%** in Q2.

### Top Churn Reasons
1. **Price sensitivity** (32% of churned customers) — SMB customers finding the product too expensive relative to their usage volume
2. **Missing features** (28%) — Most commonly cited: analytics, mobile, and SSO
3. **Competitive switch** (18%) — Primarily to DocuVerse, which offers lower pricing for basic tiers
4. **Budget cuts** (14%) — General macroeconomic belt-tightening
5. **Product not adopted internally** (8%) — Customers purchased but never achieved wide internal adoption

## Customer Satisfaction Drivers

The top three factors driving customer satisfaction:
1. **Search relevance** — Hybrid search launch in Q3 significantly improved satisfaction
2. **Customer support responsiveness** — Median first response time of 2.3 hours (target: < 4 hours)
3. **Ease of onboarding** — New onboarding wizard reduced time-to-value from 5 days to 2 days

## Customer Advisory Board Highlights

Key themes from the Q3 advisory board sessions:
- Enterprise customers strongly prefer annual contracts with multi-year discounts
- Data residency is a hard requirement for European customers, not a nice-to-have
- Customers are willing to pay a 30-40% premium for advanced AI features
- Several customers expressed interest in a "Meridian Marketplace" for third-party integrations

## Recommendations

1. Prioritize the Analytics Dashboard for Q4 — it addresses the #1 feature request and the #1 churn reason (ROI justification)
2. Invest in mobile experience for 2025
3. Explore a lighter pricing tier for SMB to reduce price-driven churn
4. Launch a Slack integration as a quick win in Q4
