# Compensation Philosophy

## Guiding Principles

Meridian Technologies is committed to a compensation framework that attracts, retains, and motivates top talent. Our total compensation philosophy is built on four principles: market competitiveness, internal equity, pay-for-performance, and transparency.

## Total Compensation Components

### Base Salary

We target the 65th percentile of market rates for comparable roles in our peer group. Peer companies include mid-stage enterprise SaaS companies with $100-300 million in ARR. We use compensation data from Radford, Pave, and Levels.fyi to benchmark annually.

Pay bands are structured by level:

| Level | Engineering | Non-Engineering |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| L3 (Junior) | $110,000 - $140,000 | $75,000 - $100,000 |
| L4 (Mid) | $140,000 - $180,000 | $100,000 - $140,000 |
| L5 (Senior) | $180,000 - $230,000 | $140,000 - $185,000 |
| L6 (Staff) | $230,000 - $290,000 | $185,000 - $240,000 |
| L7 (Principal) | $290,000 - $360,000 | $240,000 - $310,000 |
| L8 (Director+) | $320,000 - $420,000 | $280,000 - $380,000 |

### Equity

All full-time employees receive equity grants as part of their initial offer. Equity is granted as stock options (ISOs for US employees, NSOs for international) with a 4-year vesting schedule and 1-year cliff.

Initial grant values by level:

| Level | Grant Value (at last 409A) |
|-------|--------------------------|
| L3 | $40,000 - $80,000 |
| L4 | $80,000 - $160,000 |
| L5 | $160,000 - $320,000 |
| L6 | $320,000 - $500,000 |
| L7 | $500,000 - $750,000 |
| L8 | $750,000 - $1,200,000 |

Our last 409A valuation set the fair market value at $14.20 per share as of July 2024.

### Annual Refresher Grants

Employees in good standing receive annual refresher equity grants, typically 25-33% of the initial grant size. These are awarded based on performance rating and are designed to maintain competitive total compensation over time.

## Benefits

### Healthcare

We offer comprehensive health insurance covering medical, dental, and vision:
- **Medical**: Meridian covers 95% of the premium for employees, 80% for dependents
- **Dental**: Full coverage for preventive care, 80% for major dental
- **Vision**: Full coverage for annual exams and $300 allowance for frames/lenses
- **Mental Health**: Unlimited therapy sessions through our EAP partnership with Spring Health

### Other Benefits

- **401(k)**: 4% company match, immediate vesting
- **Parental Leave**: 16 weeks paid for all new parents (birth, adoption, foster)
- **PTO**: Flexible PTO policy (no fixed days, minimum 3 weeks recommended)
- **Learning & Development**: $2,500 annual stipend per employee
- **Home Office**: $1,500 one-time setup stipend, $100/month ongoing
- **Wellness**: $75/month wellness stipend (gym, fitness apps, etc.)

## Attrition and Retention

Our current voluntary attrition rate is 11.2% annualized, which is below the industry average of 15.8% for enterprise SaaS companies. Our target attrition rate is below 10%.

Attrition rate by department:
- Engineering: 9.8% (below target — strong retention)
- Sales: 16.4% (above target — addressed in Q4 retention initiative)
- Product: 7.2%
- HR: 5.0%
- G&A: 8.1%

Top reasons for voluntary departure (exit survey data):
1. Better compensation at another company (34%)
2. Career growth opportunities (28%)
3. Manager relationship (15%)
4. Work-life balance (13%)
5. Other (10%)

## Performance Reviews

Performance reviews are conducted semi-annually (January and July). Ratings follow a 5-point scale:

1. **Needs Improvement** — Not meeting expectations
2. **Developing** — Partially meeting expectations
3. **Meeting Expectations** — Solid performance
4. **Exceeding Expectations** — Consistently above expectations
5. **Exceptional** — Top 10% performer, outsized impact

Compensation adjustments (merit increases) are tied to performance ratings and typically range from 0-8% of base salary. Promotions include a base salary increase of 10-20% and an additional equity grant.
