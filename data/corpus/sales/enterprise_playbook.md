# Enterprise Sales Playbook

## Sales Methodology

Meridian Technologies uses a MEDDPICC-based sales methodology for enterprise deals (>$100K ARR). This playbook provides the framework, talk tracks, and best practices for closing enterprise opportunities.

## Deal Stages

### Stage 1: Qualification (0-10%)

**Objective**: Determine if the prospect is a good fit for Meridian.

Qualification criteria (must meet at least 3 of 5):
1. Company size: 200+ employees
2. Document volume: 10,000+ documents under management
3. Current pain: Using manual search or a legacy solution
4. Budget: Allocated budget for document intelligence/search
5. Timeline: Looking to make a decision within 6 months

**Key actions**:
- Complete initial discovery call (30 minutes)
- Identify the economic buyer and champion
- Document pain points and quantify the cost of the status quo

### Stage 2: Discovery (10-25%)

**Objective**: Deep understanding of requirements, stakeholders, and decision-making process.

**Key actions**:
- Conduct technical discovery with IT/engineering stakeholders
- Map the decision-making unit (DMU): economic buyer, champion, technical evaluator, end users
- Understand the evaluation process and competitive landscape
- Document the Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Paper process, Identified pain, Champion, Competition (MEDDPICC)

### Stage 3: Evaluation (25-50%)

**Objective**: Demonstrate Meridian's value through a guided proof of concept.

**Key actions**:
- Set up a POC environment with the customer's actual documents
- Define success criteria with the champion (e.g., "search relevance >80%", "time-to-find reduced by 50%")
- Provide a dedicated Solutions Engineer for the duration of the POC
- POC duration: 2-3 weeks (do not exceed 4 weeks)

### Stage 4: Proposal (50-75%)

**Objective**: Present pricing and negotiate terms.

**Key actions**:
- Present ROI analysis based on POC results
- Submit a formal proposal with pricing, implementation timeline, and SLAs
- Address procurement and legal requirements
- Negotiate contract terms (standard contract is 1 year with annual renewal)

### Stage 5: Closing (75-100%)

**Objective**: Execute the agreement and hand off to Customer Success.

**Key actions**:
- Final pricing approval (discounts >15% require VP Sales approval, >25% require CRO approval)
- Contract execution via DocuSign
- Internal handoff to Customer Success and Implementation teams
- Schedule kickoff call within 5 business days of signing

## Pricing Tiers

- Up to 100,000 documents
- Vector and keyword search
- Email support (24-hour response time)
- 99.9% uptime SLA

### Professional Tier — $45/user/month
- Up to 500,000 documents
- Hybrid search + reranking
- Priority support (4-hour response time)
- SSO/SAML integration
- Audit logging
- 99.95% uptime SLA

### Enterprise Tier — $75/user/month
- Unlimited documents
- All search modes + custom model support
- Dedicated Customer Success Manager
- Dedicated support channel (1-hour response time)
- Data residency options (US, EU, APAC)
- Custom SLA (up to 99.99%)

### Volume Discounts
- 100-499 users: 10% discount
- 500-999 users: 15% discount
- 1,000+ users: 20% discount
- Multi-year commitment (2+ years): additional 10% discount

## Competitive Battle Cards

### vs. DocuVerse
- **Lead with**: Superior search relevance (82% vs 64% recall), SOC 2 certification, hybrid search
- **Address**: Their lower price point — frame as "you get what you pay for" and quantify the cost of poor search relevance (time wasted per employee per day)
- **Watch for**: They offer aggressive discounts to defend deals. Our floor is 25% off list.

### vs. LegacyCorpSearch
- **Lead with**: Modern technology (AI/vector search), rapid deployment (2 days vs 3 months), better UX
- **Address**: Their compliance certifications and enterprise relationships — highlight our SOC 2 and upcoming certifications
- **Watch for**: Long-standing vendor relationships and procurement inertia. Champion must have organizational authority.
